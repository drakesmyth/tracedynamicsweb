## Selected for Website Use

### 1. "The gig workers who are training humanoid robots at home"
- **Source:** MIT Technology Review (2026 Breakthrough Technologies)
- **Date:** April 1, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/01/1134863/humanoid-data-training-gig-economy-2026-breakthrough-technology/
- **Summary:** Companies like Micro1 are recruiting thousands of workers in 50+ countries to mount cameras on their heads and film themselves performing household tasks, generating the real-world human activity data that simulation cannot produce. Investors poured over $6 billion into humanoid robots in 2025. The article highlights the emerging gig economy around robot training data collection.
- **Relevance:** Near-perfect alignment with the TRACE thesis — distributed human data collection for embodied AI training. Validates both the market need and the approach.

### 2. "In Chinese data factories, workers teach humanoid robots boring tasks"
- **Source:** Rest of World
- **Authors:** Viola Zhou, Kinling Lo
- **Date:** January 7, 2026
- **URL:** https://restofworld.org/2026/china-robots-training-centers-workers/
- **Summary:** China is building state-funded training centers — over 40 announced nationwide — where human workers perform repetitive tasks to generate movement data for humanoid robots. UBTech Robotics sold $80 million worth of humanoids to three such centers, illustrating the industrial-scale infrastructure nations are building for embodied AI data collection.
- **Relevance:** Demonstrates that real-world task data is a recognized strategic bottleneck. China is investing at national scale to solve the same problem TRACE addresses through decentralized mining.

### 3. Goldman Sachs: "The global market for robots could reach $38 billion by 2035"
- **Source:** Goldman Sachs Research
- **Date:** Late 2025 (updated forecast)
- **URL:** https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/the-global-market-for-robots-could-reach-38-billion-by-2035
- **Summary:** Goldman Sachs revised its humanoid robot total addressable market sixfold from $6B to $38B by 2035, and shipment forecasts fourfold to 1.4 million units. Key drivers: material costs fell 40% (vs. expected 15-20%), and AI progress — particularly robotic foundation models — exceeded expectations. Near-term projects 50,000-100,000 shipments in 2026.
- **Relevance:** Tier-1 investment bank validation of explosive market growth. Already referenced on the site — now with updated numbers that are significantly more bullish.

### 4. Citi Global Insights: "AI robots may outnumber workers in a few decades"
- **Source:** CNBC (reporting on Citi Global Insights research)
- **Date:** February 23, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/23/ai-robots-outnumber-workers-agents-few-decades-citi.html
- **Summary:** Rob Garlick of Citi Global Insights projects AI robots (humanoids, cleaning robots, autonomous vehicles) will reach 1.3 billion by 2035 and over 4 billion by 2050, eventually outnumbering human workers. Economic analysis shows a $15,000 humanoid achieves payback versus a $41/hour worker in under 4 weeks.
- **Relevance:** Stark capital-vs-labor math from a major financial institution. The payback period figure is particularly compelling for the investment audience.

### 5. McKinsey Global Institute: "Agents, robots, and us"
- **Source:** McKinsey Global Institute
- **Date:** November 25, 2025
- **URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/mgi/our-research/agents-robots-and-us-skill-partnerships-in-the-age-of-ai
- **Summary:** McKinsey finds that current technology could automate approximately 57% of U.S. work hours — 44% via AI agents for cognitive tasks, 13% via robots for physical tasks. Estimates approximately $2.9 trillion in economic value for the U.S. alone. Frames the future as partnerships among people, agents, and robots.
- **Relevance:** Top-tier consulting firm quantifying the automation opportunity. The 57% figure and $2.9T value are strong headline numbers for the market case.

### 6. Boston Dynamics: Production-ready Atlas unveiled at CES 2026
- **Source:** Engadget
- **Date:** January 5, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/boston-dynamics-unveils-production-ready-version-of-atlas-robot-at-ces-2026-234047882.html
- **Summary:** Boston Dynamics announced Atlas is entering production at its Boston headquarters, with all 2026 deployments committed to Hyundai and Google DeepMind. Specs: 7.5-foot reach, 110-lb lift capacity, operating range -4 to 104F. Hyundai (majority owner) plans a factory capable of producing 30,000 robots per year.
- **Relevance:** Flagship humanoid transitioning from research to production manufacturing. Hyundai's 30K/year factory plan signals industrial-scale commitment.
