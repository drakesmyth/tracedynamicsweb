The problem

Robots have no Common Crawl.

Language models read the internet. Vision models saw billions of photos. Robots have nothing close — no free, petabyte-scale record of physical human work to learn from. What exists comes from mocap studios, lab sets, and teleoperation: expensive, narrow, and missing the part that matters most — how people share space, tools, and tasks in the chaos of real work.

How it works

Wear a light kit. Record real work. Own a share.

01

Wear the kit

An MMT core — wide camera, depth, audio, motion — plus a small swarm of body sensors for full-body pose. Concealable, all-day.

02

Capture verified sessions

Go about ordinary work. You control what each session records; every hour clears automated quality and consent checks before it counts.

03

Earn a share

Every verified hour earns a proportional share of what the dataset earns from licensing — and the earliest contributors hold the largest shares.
